Why LeibTon?

I searched for a name not particularly for my site but a nickname for my social media handles. I was thinking of something that sounds nerdy. In the process came Calculus, and the two great legends who started it: Leibniz and Newton and thus I got the idea of LeibTon. This name attracted me a lot, and when I thought of making my site, I decided to name it: LeibTon.

Color Scheme of My Picture in About Page.

In my about page, I have used canvas to provide image effects using particles. The color scheme of my image matches the background.

Basically, In Canvas, Every pixel is image is rendered as a particle object and then worked upon it. If you wish to learn about it, this is an excellent Franks Laboratory video. So during rendering, I wanted to choose each pixel's RGB value such that I get a color scheme as shown in the image just above.

What I did?

Let's say the RGB value of the background is (255, 138, 128). Calculate the average of it; it comes out to be 174 (rounded).

For each pixel of the image, let the RGB value be (r, g, b). Find it's average, avg = (r+g+b)/3 Then, the final RGB of pixel to be used in canvas is calculated as: Red component = (average of rgb of pixel)(Red component of background)/(average of rgb of background). Similarly, Blue component = (average of RGB of the pixel)(Blue component of background)/(average of RGB of background) And Green component = (average of RGB of the pixel)*(Green component of background)/(average of RGB of background)

The RGB of the pixel is (avg255/174, avg138/174, avg*128/174). And we get a pleasant combination of image and background, instead of contrasting one.
